Skills File: How to Write a Blog Post

A comprehensive guide to creating engaging, effective blog content

üìö What is a Blog Post?

A blog is an online platform where individuals or organizations publish regular written content which typically features a conversational tone, allows reader interaction and covers a wide range of topics such as sports, cooking, design, living, traveling, business, and personal experiences.

Structure of a Blog Post

1. Introduction

  • Engage the reader's attention with a special hook
  • Use questions (e.g., "Are you ready?", "Have you ever wondered...?")
  • Include compelling statistics or facts
  • Share a personal anecdote or relatable scenario
  • Clearly introduce the topic and its relevance

üí° Example from "My adventures as an office intern":

"Wow! My new adventure is about to begin. Tomorrow I start my three-month internship at one of the biggest consulting companies in the world. I've been eagerly awaiting this day for over a year."

This introduction uses excitement and anticipation to hook the reader immediately.

2. Main Part

  • Organize content into paragraphs, each focusing on a specific point or argument
  • Use clear headings and subheadings to guide the reader ‚Üí better readability
  • Break up long sections to maintain reader engagement
  • Use adjectives to paint a picture and create emotions (but avoid overloading)
  • Support claims with examples, evidence, or personal experiences

üí° Example from the intern blog:

"It's hard to completely prepare for something like this, but I've tried to do my homework. Even though I've carefully read through the company's website several times, I'm feeling a little overwhelmed by all the information."

Notice the use of descriptive adjectives: "completely", "carefully", "little overwhelmed" - these create emotional connection.

3. Conclusion

  • Summarize key points without introducing new information
  • Offer recommendations or actionable advice
  • Include a call-to-action (subscribe, comment, explore related posts)
  • Leave the reader with a memorable final thought
  • End on a positive or inspiring note

üí° Example conclusion:

"*sigh* I think it's time to turn off my brain and get some sleep before my big day. Are you ready? All together now ‚Äì take a deep breath, exhale, relax and goodnight!"

This conclusion uses direct address and a calming ritual to create connection with the reader.

Language Elements

üîó Connectives

Provide smooth transitions between thoughts:

  • Additionally, Furthermore
  • However, Nevertheless
  • On the one hand
  • Therefore, Consequently
  • In fact, Indeed
  • To illustrate, For instance
  • As a result
  • Meanwhile, Subsequently

üé® Stylistic Devices

Enhance expression and appeal:

  • Metaphors: "Turn off my brain"
  • Alliteration: Repetition of sounds
  • Hyperbole: Exaggeration for effect
  • Personification: Human qualities to objects
  • Comparison: Similes and analogies
  • Repetition: Emphasizing key points
  • Imagery: Vivid descriptions

‚úçÔ∏è Language Guidelines

  • Clear and concise but also engaging
  • Avoid overly complex vocabulary
  • Use simple, straightforward language
  • Make it easy to understand
  • Balance professionalism with personality
  • Vary sentence length for rhythm

Style and Tone

Writing Styles

üí¨ Conversational

Used in lifestyle and personal blogs

  • Friendly, casual language
  • Direct address to reader ("you")
  • Personal anecdotes
  • Contractions are acceptable

üéì Formal

Used for professional and academic writing

  • Professional vocabulary
  • Structured arguments
  • Avoid slang and contractions
  • Objective tone

üìö Informative

Educating the reader about the topic

  • Clear explanations
  • Well-researched facts
  • Logical structure
  • Examples and illustrations

Tone Options

üòä Friendly

Warm, engaging, approachable

üí™ Persuasive

Confident, convincing, compelling

‚ú® Inspirational

Uplifting, motivating, encouraging

üòÑ Humorous

Light-hearted, entertaining, witty

üí° Tone Example from the intern blog:

The tone is friendly and conversational with touches of humor and self-deprecation: "Will I make any mistakes? Who will I eat lunch with? Are my clothes okay? Will people understand my English or will they just ignore me if they don't understand what I'm saying??"

Target Audience

Adjust your writing style according to your target audience:

For Professionals

  • Use formal language
  • Provide in-depth analysis
  • Include industry-specific terminology
  • Focus on data and evidence
  • Maintain professional tone throughout

For General Audience

  • Adopt a more conversational tone
  • Simplify complex concepts
  • Use relatable examples
  • Avoid jargon or explain technical terms
  • Make content accessible to all

üí° Pro Tip:

Always consider who will be reading your blog. Ask yourself: What does my audience already know? What do they need to learn? What language will resonate with them?

The Power of Adjectives

Adjectives help paint a picture for the reader and create emotions, but avoid overloading your writing.

Effective Use of Adjectives

  • Specific over generic: Instead of "big company" ‚Üí "biggest consulting company"
  • Emotional connection: "eagerly awaiting", "incredibly excited"
  • Vivid descriptions: "carefully read", "little overwhelmed", "huge organisation"
  • Balanced approach: Don't use too many in one sentence

üí° Examples from the intern blog:

  • "biggest consulting companies" - emphasizes scale
  • "eagerly awaiting" - shows enthusiasm
  • "incredibly excited" - intensifies emotion
  • "multinational company" - provides context
  • "little, lonely intern" - creates sympathy and humor
  • "helpful advice" - adds value

Crafting Compelling Headlines

Your headline is the first thing readers see - make it count!

Headline Best Practices

  • Be clear and descriptive: Tell readers exactly what they'll learn
  • Use action words: "Discover", "Learn", "Master", "Transform"
  • Include numbers: "5 Ways to...", "10 Tips for..."
  • Create curiosity: Make readers want to know more
  • Keep it concise: Aim for 6-12 words
  • Use keywords: For SEO and clarity

üí° Example:

"My adventures as an office intern" - This headline is personal, intriguing, and promises a narrative story rather than dry information.

‚úÖ Blog Post Checklist

  • Engaging introduction with a hook
  • Clear structure with headings and subheadings
  • Each paragraph focuses on one main idea
  • Smooth transitions using connectives
  • Appropriate use of adjectives for emotion and description
  • Style and tone match target audience
  • Stylistic devices enhance (not overwhelm) the content
  • Clear, concise language throughout
  • Strong conclusion with call-to-action
  • Proofread for grammar and spelling
  • Compelling headline that attracts readers

üéØ Final Tips for Success

Ask questions: As the intern blog shows, asking questions engages readers and shows vulnerability: "Will I make any mistakes? Who will I eat lunch with?"

Show personality: Let your unique voice shine through. The intern's nervous excitement and self-deprecating humor make the blog relatable.

Be authentic: Share real experiences, emotions, and insights. Readers connect with genuine content.

Edit ruthlessly: First drafts are never perfect. Review, revise, and refine your work.
